Chicken Curry Recipe

Chicken curry is a flavorful and aromatic dish that holds a special place in many culinary traditions across the globe. Originating in the Indian subcontinent, this dish has evolved into countless regional and international variations, each with its unique blend of spices, herbs, and cooking techniques. At its core, chicken curry typically features tender pieces of chicken simmered in a rich, spiced gravy made from ingredients like onions, garlic, ginger, tomatoes, and a variety of spices such as turmeric, cumin, coriander, and garam masala.

Over time, chicken curry has transcended cultural boundaries, becoming a beloved comfort food in countries like the UK, Thailand, Malaysia, and the Caribbean. Whether enjoyed with rice, naan, or roti, chicken curry offers a deeply satisfying and warming experience, perfect for both everyday meals and festive occasions.

Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Tomatoes
  • Oil or Ghee
  • Yogurt or Coconut Milk (optional)
  • Water or Stock

Spices

  • Turmeric powder
  • Coriander powder
  • Cumin poder or seeds
  • Red chili powder
  • Garam masala
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. Prep the Ingredients:
    1. Cut chicken into medium-sized pieces.
    2. Finely chop onions, tomatoes, garlic, and ginger (or use paste).
    3. easure out all spices and keep them ready.
  2. Heat Oil and Sauté Aromatics:
    1. In a deep pan or pot, heat 2–3 tbsp of oil or ghee over medium heat.
    2. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown (this can take 8–10 minutes).
    3. Add garlic and ginger; sauté for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add Tomatoes and Spices:
    1. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and the oil starts to separate (5–7 minutes).
    2. Stir in the spices:
      • 1 tsp turmeric
      • 1–2 tsp coriander powder
      • 1 tsp cumin powder
      • 1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
      • Salt to taste
    3. Cook for another 2–3 minutes, stirring to prevent burning.
  4. Add Chicken:
    1. Add the chicken pieces to the pan and stir to coat them well with the masala (spice mix).
    2. Sear the chicken for 5–6 minutes until lightly browned on all sides.
  5. Add Liquid and Simmer:
    1. Add 1 to 1.5 cups of water (or chicken stock) to the pan.
    2. Stir well, bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low.
    3. Cover and simmer for 20–25 minutes or until chicken is cooked through and tender.
  6. Final Touches:
    1. Add 1 tsp garam masala and stir.
    2. Simmer uncovered for 5 more minutes to thicken the curry.
    3. Taste and adjust salt or spice as needed.
  7. Garnish and Serve:
    1. Garnish with fresh coriander (cilantro).
    2. Serve hot with steamed rice, naan, or roti.
